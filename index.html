About

Hello! I showcase this picture because I believe we should all have humility and fun with what we do! This was an event our management team made to allow co-workers to throw pies in our faces if they met the quota! My journey began as mentioned prior in my grandma's store. I began viewing a lot of work differently than most people as to how to make it most productive and what information could I obtain to make the most impact. An example of this was my first job out of high school at Sears. I was a stock-boy helping show off our awesome products and I noticed our rainchecks were barely ever used and not very many people called anyone! I was curious one day and asked a few people why that was the case and they told me it was never organized. I fixed the issue and just started calling customers in my free time when done with my work. I learned then it was always great to be curious and have your eyes open for new opportunities for success.

I used this at Panera Bread with our WhenToManage software which is a mixture of many softwares to bring full view of the resturant and help us look at KPI's. I used this software as a shift manager to produce shifts in enthusiasm in our team by sharing our success as well as sharing our needed ways to improve. Ultimately, knowing the job so well I made charts that assiocates could use if they were new to the job and how I ran my shifts with time layouts. Both of these shifts helped our team succeed and reach the secound fastest store in the coutnry for 6 months and counting!

This lead me to my next job where I began driving truck to support my new family and my daughter! While in training I noticed we were using 3 ring binders and it had all our stop info and extra information we could use. But it wasn't very great at being accurate for entering the facilities. So I was talking with my driver trainner the one day and I mentioned if they knew about Dropbox. This I teamed up with my driver trainner to help gather data for customers and plug it into the system. Meanwhile we found we could link videos and show exactly where they could enter the facilities and saved the company plenty of time and money, especially for new drivers. One wrong turn in New York City could mean 30 minutes of traffic to turn around.

While I am proud of my accomplishments, I was lucky enough to join Thinkful's Data Analytics Immersion program which, looking back on my life I think is the perfect fit. I now get to use the normal mindset I had while doing jobs in the field and make a real impact. The skills I gained in this course allow me to use data and pair it with my love for analytics and making real impact's at a company. I pride myself in being a problem solver and I think this is one of the most important roles any assiocate could have. I believe any stage of the ladder you are in a company making an impact and solving real problems for the company is most important.
